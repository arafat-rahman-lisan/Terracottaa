Dream Catchers

Handmade dream catchers with woven webs and feathers, perfect for bedroom décor, gifting, and spiritual or calm interior spaces.

Cultural Background & Story

In rural Bangladesh, handcrafted hanging ornaments have long been part of everyday household life. They are traditionally placed near windows, doors, or sleeping spaces as symbols of protection, peace, and positive energy. The circular weaving of threads reflects a broader Bengali tradition of symbolic hand-weaving, where patterns are created slowly and with intention rather than haste. These dream catchers are made by artisans who patiently weave cotton threads into delicate web-like forms, carefully attaching natural feathers and beads. The process requires steady hands, rhythm, and deep concentration, skills developed over years of practice within artisan families.

Tradition & Legacy

This craft is rooted in Bangladesh’s Kutir Shilpo (cottage industry) culture, where artistry is learned at home and passed quietly from one generation to the next, woven naturally into daily life rather than separated from it.
